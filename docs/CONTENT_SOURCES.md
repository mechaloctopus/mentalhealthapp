# Content & Sources — the Inner Path curriculum

Every wisdom path is drawn from real source material and offered as **optional
contemplative practice** — "explore what resonates" — never as religious instruction,
doctrine, or a substitute for professional care. In-app, each path surfaces its concept,
how we translate it into practice, and this attribution (see `src/side/pathContext.ts`).

## Paths & sources

### The Five Temples — Gospel of Mary
- **Source:** The Gospel of Mary (Mary of Magdala). Survives in a 5th-c. Coptic manuscript,
  the **Berlin Codex (Papyrus Berolinensis 8502)**, with two earlier Greek fragments
  (**Papyrus Rylands 463**, **P. Oxyrhynchus 3525**). Usually dated to the **2nd century**;
  roughly half the text is lost.
- **Scholarship:** Karen L. King, *The Gospel of Mary of Magdala: Jesus and the First Woman
  Apostle* (2003).
- **Themes used:** matter dissolving to its root; "there is no sin" inherent in us; the Good
  restoring every nature to its root; "do not lay down rules… lest you be constrained";
  the soul's ascent answering the Powers/Authorities (Darkness, Desire/Craving, Ignorance,
  and the seven-form Wrath); the soul's attainment of rest. The Five Temples (Truth,
  Self-Knowledge, Liberation, Inner Mastery, Union) map this ascent into daily practice.

### The Seven Habits
- **Source:** Stephen R. Covey, *The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People* (1989).

### Acts of Stewardship (Purpose)
- **Sources:** Viktor Frankl, *Man's Search for Meaning* (1946) & logotherapy; behavioral
  activation in modern clinical psychology; service ethics across traditions.

### The Way of Mushin (Flow)
- **Sources:** Zen/budō "mushin no shin"; Takuan Sōhō, *The Unfettered Mind* (17th c.);
  Eugen Herrigel, *Zen in the Art of Archery* (1948); Mihály Csíkszentmihályi, *Flow* (1990).

### Heart Coherence
- **Sources:** Heart-rate-variability & resonance-breathing research (HeartMath Institute;
  Lehrer & Gevirtz on resonance-frequency breathing), popularized by Gregg Braden. Framed
  strictly as wellness practice, **not** medical treatment.

### The Bodhisattva Path
- **Sources:** Mahāyāna Buddhism; Śāntideva, *Bodhicaryāvatāra* (8th c.); Tibetan *lojong*
  mind-training (tonglen). Always optional, never required.

### The Stoic Path
- **Sources:** Marcus Aurelius, *Meditations* (2nd c.); Epictetus, *Enchiridion* &
  *Discourses*; Seneca, *Letters to Lucilius*.

### The Wisdom Library
- **Sources:** the Upanishads (c. 800–200 BCE); the Diamond Sūtra (*Vajracchedikā*); the
  Lotus Sūtra (*Saddharma-puṇḍarīka*); the Mahāmudrā tradition; Advaita / non-dual teachings.

## Daily practices
Universal daily practices draw from: resonance/coherence breathing; positive-psychology
gratitude; metta (loving-kindness); ānāpānasati (breath awareness); self-inquiry
(Ramana Maharshi, *ātma vichāra*); maraṇasati / *memento mori* (impermanence);
forgiveness practice; and the Golden Rule.

## Editorial principles
1. **Practice over consumption** — every concept becomes a small action, reflection, or habit.
2. **Honest attribution** — sources are named with dates; distillations are clearly summaries.
3. **Non-dogmatic & inclusive** — presented as paths many have found meaningful; the user
   chooses what resonates.
4. **Humble claims** — clear boundaries between wellness/contemplative support and medical
   treatment; crisis resources surfaced where appropriate.
